## Key Insights

**Data Room Preparation**
- Assembling complete data room before signing term sheet cuts 1 week off closing process
- Series A closing typically takes >1 month, much spent tracking down documents for lawyers

**Critical Document Categories**
- Corporate documents: All board/shareholder minutes, incorporation certificates, bylaws, organizational charts
- Financial: Current business plan, projections, recent financial statements
- IP portfolio: Complete list of trademarks, patents, copyrights, domains with filing documentation
- Cap table: Full stockholder list with issuance dates/prices, option holder details with grant dates/exercise prices
- Legal agreements: Any contracts >$25K, real estate leases, debt documents, insurance policies
- Employment: Employee/consultant list with titles, salaries, benefits, classification, residence state
- Benefit plans: Stock option plans, 401(k), pension, insurance with all related forms and filings

**Equity Grant Timing**
- Complete all pending equity grants BEFORE starting Series A process
- Term sheet receipt triggers material event requiring new 409A valuation
- Late equity grants force higher strike prices, reducing employee upside
- May require compensation increases to avoid morale issues

**Compliance Requirements**
- Securities law exemptions (Rule 701, state blue sky laws)
- Employee classification documentation (exempt/non-exempt status)
- Foreign employee benefits listed separately by country

## Bottom Line
Prepare a comprehensive data room with all corporate, financial, legal, and employment documents before signing your Series A term sheet to accelerate closing and avoid equity grant complications.
