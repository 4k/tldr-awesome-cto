## Key Insights

- Writing becomes critical when engineering teams exceed "a few dozen people" - face-to-face communication breaks down at scale
- Writing enables engineers to influence beyond immediate peers, essential for progression from senior to staff/principal/distinguished levels
- Key documents to make durable: proposals, decisions, coding guidelines, best practices, runbooks, debugging guides, postmortems, code reviews
- Well-written content scales communication efficiency across multiple teams and organizations
- Fundamental improvement tactics: ruthlessly re-edit your own writing, put yourself in reader's shoes, challenge yourself to deliver same message in shorter form
- Recommended tools: Grammarly and Hemingway App for immediate feedback
- Recommended books: "On Writing Well" and "The Sense of Style"
- Get feedback from colleagues who write well

## Bottom Line

Writing well is an undervalued but essential skill that enables senior engineers to scale their influence beyond immediate teams as organizations grow beyond face-to-face communication limits.
