# Career Growth Frameworks in Software Engineering: A Review

**Source:** https://web.archive.org/web/20210123114037/https://medium.com/better-programming/career-growth-frameworks-in-software-engineering-a-review-4aa6c59a9cf6
**Section:** Career growth

---

## Key Insights

**Historical Context & Problems**
- Career ladders stem from Taylorism/Fordism industrial models that treated workers as replaceable cogs - incompatible with knowledge work
- Peter Principle: employees get promoted to their level of incompetence
- Dual-track ladders (management vs technical) fail because technical roles still viewed as inferior "workers"

**Motivation Framework**
- External rewards (pay, titles) cease motivating once basic fairness achieved and can actually diminish performance
- Three intrinsic motivators: Mastery, Autonomy, Purpose (Daniel Pink's "Drive")
- Quote: "Tell me how you measure me, and I will tell you how I will behave"

**Developer Performance Measurement**
- Individual developer productivity cannot be objectively measured - all attempts fail
- Metrics like lines of code, hours worked, story points useful for teams/projects but useless for individual assessment
- Software engineers are architects, not brick layers - their goal is building product, not writing code

**Company Approaches**
- Netflix: Flat structure, no formal framework
- Spotify: Delayed framework 8 years (admits too long), then skill matrix
- Google: Dual-track works at scale, flat structure failed spectacularly
- Medium: Abandoned holacracy due to overhead/scaling issues

**Common Patterns**
- Skill matrices nearly universal among progressive companies
- Separate growth frameworks from external titles
- Growth frameworks inseparable from company culture
- Level reflects abilities, doesn't define them

**Implementation Framework**
1. Define company values first - culture is what you do, not what you say
2. Decide salary transparency and title approach upfront (hardest to retrofit)
3. Regular 1:1 meetings - nothing in performance reviews should surprise
4. Fair competency matrix describing tasks/behaviors (not metrics)
5. Defined compensation review process

**Key Trade-offs**
- Hierarchy depth vs flatness
- Titles vs roles vs no-titles  
- Centralized vs distributed decision-making
- Open vs closed salaries
- Early framework vs delayed implementation
- Conventional vs experimental approaches

## Bottom Line
Skip complex performance measurement systems and focus on regular feedback through competency matrices that support intrinsic motivation (mastery, autonomy, purpose) rather than trying to objectively rank individual developer productivity.
