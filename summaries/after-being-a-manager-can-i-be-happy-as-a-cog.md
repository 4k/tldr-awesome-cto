# After Being A Manager, Can I Be Happy As A Cog?

**Source:** https://charity.wtf/2019/11/23/questionable-advice-after-being-a-manager-can-i-be-happy-as-a-cog/
**Section:** People Management

---

## Key Insights

**Career Transition Reality:**
- After 2 years managing, you have ~2-3 more years before returning to IC becomes very difficult
- Senior ICs who've been managers wield outsized influence through "shadow management" and strategic advice
- Smart managers lean heavily on ex-manager ICs for mentorship, strategy, and organizational insight

**Management vs IC Power Dynamics:**
- Most orgs don't actively push back against power flowing from engineers to managers
- Going back to IC will feel like losing status/influence because in most places, it actually is
- Technical decisions should belong to technical people, but this only happens when senior ICs actively assert ownership

**Strategic Approach for Ex-Manager ICs:**
- Use management experience to organize other senior ICs to claim technical decision-making authority
- Leverage new "mystical aura" and leadership connections to advocate for better IC empowerment
- Target companies that use ICs at strategic/executive levels, not just tactical roles

**Organizational Change Framework:**
- Senior ICs must drive change in IC empowerment - managers won't give power they don't have to
- Model fluid movement between IC/management roles without promotion/demotion baggage
- Fight inherited command-and-control structures by creating better ones daily

**Age/Skills Concerns:**
- Technical skills are "the easy part" - business understanding and influence matter more
- 25 years of experience trumps kubernetes knowledge from 20-somethings

## Bottom Line
Use your management experience as a superpower to become an influential senior IC who shapes how organizations balance technical and people leadership, rather than seeing it as a step backward.
