# The Secret To Discussing Pay With Employees

**Source:** https://www.officevibe.com/blog/secret-to-discussing-pay-with-employees
**Section:** People Management

---

## Key Insights

**Context is Critical**
- 82% of employees believe they're paid below market rate (most are wrong due to lack of context)
- 44% of employees who feel underpaid actually earn market rate or above
- Employees create their own narratives about pay when context is missing, leading to resentment

**3 Core Areas Managers Must Understand**
1. **Company Compensation Philosophy** - Link pay decisions to company values/goals to answer "why this amount?"
2. **Market Positioning** - Know how company sets ranges, stays competitive, and compares to competitors
3. **Salary Adjustment Process** - Understand frameworks for increases, promotions, bonuses, role changes

**Communication Framework**
- Decouple pay conversations from performance reviews
- Tie performance to development/growth, pay to market standards
- Provide non-monetary recognition alongside financial explanations
- Train managers on compensation rationale before they communicate numbers

**Key Statistics**
- Pay transparency issues cause more departures than actual low pay
- Fair compensation is baseline requirement before employees prioritize growth/purpose
- Lack of context drives perceived unfairness even when compensation is market-appropriate

**Implementation**
- Managers should request proper training on compensation reasoning from HR
- Stay current on industry market data throughout the year
- Position compensation as story of how organization values employees

## Bottom Line
Pay communication failures stem from lack of context, not actual compensation levels - train managers on the "why" behind numbers to prevent employee turnover driven by perceived unfairness.
