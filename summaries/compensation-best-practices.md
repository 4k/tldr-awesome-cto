# Compensation Best Practices

**Source:** https://www.payscale.com/cbpr
**Section:** People Management

---

## Key Insights

- **Voluntary turnover dropped to 13% in 2024** - cooling labor market giving employers more leverage over workers
- **Workplace flexibility correlates with retention** - median turnover rates lower for organizations with flexible policies
- **47% of organizations report tension between fair pay and cost optimization** - direct result of economic uncertainty and labor market power shift
- **Pay increases declining: 3.5% planned for 2025 vs 3.8% given in 2024** - roughly half of organizations actively reducing compensation spend
- **Pay equity analysis adoption surged to 57% vs 38% in 2020** - despite broader DEI pullback (only 11% plan to reduce DEI initiatives)
- **Pay transparency slightly declining: 56% publish ranges in 2025 vs 60% in 2024** - but organizations still want more transparency than current levels
- **Compensation strategy adoption hit majority for first time** - up from less than 1/3 in 2020 to majority in 2025
- **AI replacement intent: 18% actively replacing workers, 20% considering it** - 52% have no plans
- **Compensation maturity framework**: Only 1/3 of organizations are mature (Advancing/Optimizing stages), 2/3 still developing
- **Purpose-built compensation software strongly linked to advanced maturity stages** - lack of technology blocks maturity progress

## Bottom Line
Organizations are cutting pay increases and leveraging a cooler labor market while simultaneously investing in compensation strategy, pay equity analysis, and technology to manage growing tensions between cost control and fair pay.
