# Increment: Teams

**Source:** https://increment.com/teams/
**Section:** People Management

---

## Key Insights

- **Small team focus**: Twilio scaled from 3 to 2,300 engineers by anchoring growth around small teams as the core organizational unit
- **Compensation equity framework**: Implement systematic pay audits and correction processes to prevent compensation inequity before it becomes entrenched
- **Distributed team systems**: Use deliberate communication systems (including emoji protocols) and structured processes to maintain efficiency across geographic boundaries
- **Engineering manager technical requirements**: Technical depth matters less than ability to make sound technical decisions and earn team credibility
- **Interview process redesign**: Shift from "culture fit" to inclusion-focused interviews that promote positivity and assess actual job-relevant skills
- **Value over volume metrics**: Reward engineering impact and problem-solving capability rather than lines of code or feature count
- **Human factors dominate quality**: Studies show team dynamics and human factors influence software quality more than technical tooling choices
- **Cross-functional skill integration**: Diverse skill sets (design, copywriting, domain expertise) significantly improve software project outcomes
- **Editorial process adoption**: Engineering teams can learn from editorial organizations' paper-and-lore-based processes that consistently ship on schedule
- **Management transition framework**: Provide structured paths and training for developers moving from individual contributor to engineering management roles

## Bottom Line

Focus on small team structures, human-centered processes, and systematic equity practices rather than purely technical solutions when scaling engineering organizations.
