# Financial Planning & Analysis @ GitLab

**Source:** https://about.gitlab.com/handbook/finance/financial-planning-and-analysis/
**Section:** Money / Finance

---

## Key Insights

**FP&A Structure & Process:**
- 5 sub-teams supporting unified FP&A mission: maximize long-term valuation through aligned execution
- Monthly 8-day accounting close target with full consolidation by working day 10
- Weekly FP&A/TA interlock process drives 50/50 "most realistic" headcount forecasting
- Position ID (PID) system creates 1:1 relationship between approved headcount and hiring plan

**Planning Cycles:**
- Annual Operating Plan (AOP): 3-statement bottoms-up plan spanning current fiscal year, board-approved
- Long Range Outlook (LRO): 3-5 year strategic plan refreshed annually, focuses on growth curve drivers
- Rolling List of Asks (RLOA): Quarterly reinvestment process tied to beating expectations

**Key Timelines:**
- FY26 AOP milestones span Sep 2024 - Mar 2025 with final board approval
- Monthly forecast locks by working day 9
- Quarterly guidance process follows accounting close with CFO/IR review
- RLOA decisions made by week 4 of each quarter

**Performance Targets:**
- Revenue and EBIT variance within +/- 2% quarterly vs plan/forecast
- 90% coverage target for variance explanations
- Department expenses within +/- 2% vs quarterly forecast

**Control Mechanisms:**
- Procure-to-pay workflow as primary expense control
- Weekly P&L snapshots in months 2-3 of fiscal quarters
- Monthly variance meetings with EVPs held accountable to dollar budgets
- Business planning required for top growth drivers with cross-functional accountability

## Bottom Line
GitLab's FP&A operates a rigorous planning machine with weekly headcount interlocks, quarterly reinvestment cycles, and tight variance controls targeting 2% accuracy to drive predictable execution of long-term strategy.
