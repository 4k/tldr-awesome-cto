# Year 5 Update

**Source:** https://miguelcarranza.es/cto-year-5
**Section:** CTO Position

---

## Key Insights

**Team Growth & Structure:**
- Shifted from 1 cross-functional team to 7 teams (+600%)
- Engineering team grew 47% to 25 people (vs 100% planned)
- Conservative hiring in Q3 due to macro conditions was correct decision
- Female engineer representation improved from 18% to 27%

**Process Maturation:**
- Implemented OKRs, 30/60/90 planning, customer triaging, cross-team calibrations
- Established mature on-call processes with runbooks and shadowing
- Made Zendesk the unified OS for support tickets to eliminate context switching

**CTO Role Evolution:**
- Replaced weekly 1:1s with open office hours for entire company
- Shifted from leading technical projects to technical consultant role
- Took support engineering under CTO (technical team reporting to technical leader)
- Support team split into "self-serve" vs "sold" tracks

**Support Engineering Fixes:**
- Unified all ticket sources into Zendesk
- Made support more technical with iOS dev sessions and pair programming
- Data-driven approach to reduce common tickets through better docs/product/tools
- Quickly improved morale and response times

**2023 Strategy - "Year of Shipping":**
- New GTM engineering team for same-day customer implementations
- Focus on product improvements for larger accounts
- More selective hiring with limited headcount budget

**Key Learnings:**
- First vested employees stayed (growth mindset > early-stage nostalgia)
- Office hours recovered calendar time but requires proactive scheduling for key people
- Founders must stay connected to users through sales/support/interviews
- Founder workload never disappears - institutional knowledge sharing is irreplaceable

## Bottom Line
Mature processes and strong team structure enabled the founder CTO to shift from operational bottleneck to strategic technical consultant while maintaining customer connection through support engineering ownership.
