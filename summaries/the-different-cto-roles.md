# The Different CTO Roles

**Source:** https://www.allthingsdistributed.com/2007/07/the_different_cto_roles.html
**Section:** CTO Position

---

## Key Insights
- Four distinct CTO models based on scope and focus:
  - **Infrastructure Manager**: Handles data centers, networks, security when CIO role becomes too complex (traditional businesses)
  - **Technology Visionary + Operations Manager**: Determines tech strategy AND runs operations (dot-coms, tech companies, often co-founders)
  - **External Facing Technologist**: Bridge between customers and internal dev, drives product portfolio (software/middleware companies)
  - **Big Thinker**: Evaluates tech for new business models, competitive analysis, disruption prevention

- Management span varies dramatically by model:
  - Models 1-2: Direct management of 500-1000+ engineers
  - Models 3-4: Small teams of 10-50 engineers functioning as tech incubators

- Influence structure differs:
  - First two models: Influence through direct engineering org control
  - Last two models: Must influence across divisions, typically report to CEO

- Role emerged in late 1980s when R&D lab directors moved to strategic positions to capitalize on lab results

- No universal CTO definition exists - role depends entirely on company type and technology's strategic importance

## Bottom Line
The CTO role has four distinct patterns based on whether you manage large engineering teams directly or influence strategy across the organization with smaller incubation teams.
