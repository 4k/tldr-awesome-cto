## Key Insights

**Business & Strategy**
- Understand what makes money - only business value matters long-term
- Design systems for appropriate scale/scope, avoid over-engineering
- Take high-risk, high-reward projects for career acceleration
- Question everything with repetitive "why" until reaching root causes

**Leadership & Influence**
- Maintain high hiring bar and get involved in recruitment
- Lead at least one major project with clear success metrics
- Demand accountability and ownership from team members
- Work to increase influence across multiple teams
- Practice extreme ownership principles

**Technical Growth**
- Develop deep expertise in your team's core technologies
- Expand breadth in both domain knowledge and general technology
- Build small prototypes to explore emerging tech
- Read technical books annually
- Thoroughly understand pros/cons before suggesting new production technologies

**Career Management**
- Ask manager for stretch projects or help identify them
- Align your work with manager's goals explicitly
- Drive your own 1:1s with prepared topic lists
- Present problems to manager with potential solutions ready
- Secure strong mentors for navigation and growth

**Collaboration**
- Listen to others, accept multiple valid solutions exist
- Participate in multiple projects as consultant/reviewer/mentor
- Mentor 2+ junior engineers
- Network effectively across all levels (seniors, peers, juniors)
- Work to disambiguate ambiguous problem statements

## Bottom Line
Senior engineers must balance deep technical expertise with business acumen, leadership skills, and strategic career management across multiple concurrent projects and relationships.
