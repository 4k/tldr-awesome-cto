# Hug your manager

**Source:** https://buttondown.email/cote/archive/hug-your-manager/
**Section:** People Management

---

## Key Insights

- **Managers as organization builders**: Beyond process enforcement, managers continuously build, define, and iterate on organizational structures—like product managers building apps
- **Agile vs. waterfall management**: Organizations that use iterative management (test theories, adapt, change) outperform those creating static "final" org charts upfront
- **Management release cycles**: Approximately 6-month cycles for organizational changes and adaptations
- **Invisible workload**: Most critical management work (building org structure, mediating conflicts, setting strategy) is least visible to employees
- **Continuous management needed**: Organizations require constant adjustment and "production support" like software systems—managers wear organizational "pagers"
- **Employee perspective gap**: Individual contributors often assume managers have complete plans when they're actually operating like product teams with evolving backlogs
- **Culture isn't execution**: Good culture is necessary but insufficient—actual management work (structure, procedure, policy) is the execution engine
- **Upward recursion of pressure**: Every level has someone above breathing down their neck, from employees to managers to executives to shareholders
- **Management transparency opportunity**: Managers should run retrospectives and post-mortems on organizational changes, sharing lessons learned with teams

**Specific Framework**: Treat organizational management like product development—start with theories, test in short cycles, iterate based on learning, maintain continuous improvement rather than big-bang reorganizations.

## Bottom Line
Most managers are actually running continuous product development cycles on the organization itself, but this critical work remains invisible to employees who should recognize and support these efforts.
